Well lectures are on lecture notes, so just add more notes on vector bundles.

Direct sum of two vector bundles \(p_1:E_1\to B\) and \(p_2:E_2\to B\) is \[E_1\oplus E_2=\{(v_1,v_2)\in E_1\times E_2\ |\ p_1(v_1)=p_2(v_2)\}\] it's a bit confusing though.

7.23

Finally have some time to sit down to do some work.

Some examples of vector bundles: trivial bundle \(E=B\times\mathbb{R}^n\) with projection.

Mobius bundle: \(I\times\mathbb{R}/(0,t)\sim(1,-t)\)

Tangent bundle (of \(S^n\subseteq\mathbb{R}^{n+1}\)), Normal bundle(of \(S^n\subseteq\mathbb{R}^{n+1}\)), Canonical line bundle (of \(\mathbb{R}P^n\)), Orthogonal complement of the canonical line bundle (of \(\mathbb{R}P^n\)).

Grassmann manifold \(G_k(\mathbb{R}^n)\)

isomorphism between vector bundles is a homeo where it's linear isomorphism on each bundle.

Section of vector bundle \(E\) is \(s:B\to E\) such that \(ps=id_B\)

Examples of sections: zero sections and nonvanishing sections.

They can be used to tell if two vector bundles are isomorphic.

6.19

G=\(<x,y,z|x^2=1,y^2=1,z^2=\frac{1}{2}(1+x+y-xy),xy=yx,xz=zy,yz=zx>\)​​ is the presentation of a group algebra. something something I forgot (\(z^2\) part).

Then \(G=span\{1,x,y,z,xy,xz,yz,xyz\}\).

And we quotient out the left ideal generated by \((1-y)\) namely \(G(1-y)\).

We can identify the ideal to be \((1-y)=span\{1-y,x-xy,z-zy,yz-xyz\}\)

And the quotient would be \(G/(1-y)=span\{1,x,z,yz\}\).

The question was raised by Yuhan and the main issue here is that although we quotient out 1-y, we cannot simply replace y with 1. (Actually important)

6.18

"Proved" elliptic curves (\(y^2=x^3+ax+b\) where \(4a^3+27b^2\ne0\)) is topologically a torus in \(\mathbb{C}P^2\). Not exactly but used complex regular value theorem and Riemann-Hurwitz to show it's isomorphic to \(T^2\backslash\{*\}\). This question popped up randomly when Dougal told me about elliptic integrals and we looked up a little on elliptic curves.

Let me write down the definition of a vector bundle for the first time:

An n-dimensional vector bundle is a map \(p:E\to B\) together with a real vector space structure on \(p^{-1}(b)\) for each \(b\in B\), such that the following local triviality condition is satisfied: There is a open cover of B by open sets \(U_\alpha\), for each of them there exists a homeomorphism \(h_\alpha:p^{-1}(U_\alpha)\to U_\alpha\times\mathbb{R}^n\) taking \(p^{-1}(b)\) to \(\{b\}\times\mathbb{R}^n\) by a vector space isomorphism for each \(b\in U_\alpha\).
